ğŸ’¡ Practical Tips

You don't need a design degree. These seven PowerPoint-specific tips will immediately improve every chart you present.

  1. Start with a built-in template, then strip it down

    Insert a chart using PowerPoint's built-in templates. Then remove every element that doesn't directly support your message.

  2. Remove the legend â€” label bars directly

    Click on the legend and delete it. Instead, right-click a data series â†’ Add Data Labels. Direct labeling eliminates the back-and-forth eye movement between legend and bars.

  3. Use Quick Layout 1 (minimal)

    Click the chart â†’ Chart Design tab â†’ Quick Layout â†’ Layout 1. This gives you the cleanest starting point.

  4. Delete gridlines

    Click on any gridline to select them â†’ press Delete. Gridlines add visual noise without adding information when your bars are labeled.

  5. One accent color + grey

    Pick a single accent color for the bar or line you want to highlight. Make everything else a neutral grey. This instantly creates a visual hierarchy.

  6. Minimum 14pt font size

    If someone in the back of the room can't read your axis labels, they don't exist. Set all chart text to at least 14pt.

  7. Add a story-driven title

    Don't title your chart "Revenue by Quarter." Instead, use a text box above the chart that says "Revenue grew 12% in Q4, driven by enterprise sales." Tell them the insight, not the axis.
