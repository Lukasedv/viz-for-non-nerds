Edward Tufte introduced the concept of the data-ink ratio: the proportion of a graphic's ink devoted to the non-redundant display of data. Data-ink is the non-erasable core â€” remove it, and you lose information. Everything else is potentially "chart junk."

As the team at Dark Horse Analytics put it: "Data Looks Better Naked." The goal is to maximize the data-ink ratio by stripping away non-data elements that clutter without informing.

Common offenders

  • 3D effects and perspective distortion
  • Heavy, prominent gridlines
  • Background images or colors
  • Gradient fills on bars or areas
  • Shadow and bevel effects
  • Decorative clip-art and icons
  • Unnecessary borders and boxes
